SAT Constancia de Situación Fiscal

Complete guide to Mexico's tax status certificate

The SAT Constancia de Situación Fiscal is the most important document for verifying Mexican businesses. This official certificate from Mexico's Tax Administration Service (SAT) confirms a taxpayer's current fiscal status and is essential for KYC compliance.

Quick Summary

The SAT Constancia contains: RFC (tax ID), legal name, fiscal address, tax regime, economic activities (CFDI codes), and tax obligations. It's the authoritative source for a business's tax registration status in Mexico.

What is a SAT Constancia?

The Constancia de Situación Fiscal is an official document issued by the Servicio de Administración Tributaria (SAT), Mexico's tax authority. It certifies that a person or business is registered in the Federal Taxpayer Registry (RFC) and shows their current tax situation.

For KYC purposes, the SAT Constancia serves as:

  • Primary verification of business existence and registration
  • Source of truth for the RFC (tax ID number)
  • Official record of the fiscal address
  • Confirmation of active tax status

RFC Format and Validation

The RFC (Registro Federal de Contribuyentes) follows specific formats:

For Companies (Personas Morales)

12 characters: XXX000000XX0

  • 3 letters from company name
  • 6 digits for founding date (YYMMDD)
  • 3-character homoclave (verification code)

For Individuals (Personas Físicas)

13 characters: XXXX000000XXX

  • 4 letters from name (paternal surname, maternal surname, first name)
  • 6 digits for birth date (YYMMDD)
  • 3-character homoclave (verification code)

RFC Validation Tips

When validating an RFC:
✓ Check correct length (12 or 13 characters)
✓ Verify date portion is valid
✓ Confirm against SAT database for active status
✓ Cross-reference name encoding matches legal name

Fiscal Address (Domicilio Fiscal)

The fiscal address on the SAT Constancia is the official registered address for tax purposes. It includes:

  • Calle - Street name
  • Número Exterior - External number
  • Número Interior - Internal number (if applicable)
  • Colonia - Neighborhood/colony
  • Código Postal - Postal code
  • Municipio/Delegación - Municipality
  • Estado - State

For KYC purposes, the fiscal address takes precedence over addresses on other documents. However, businesses may operate from different locations, so it's important to also verify operational addresses through proof of address documents.

How to Validate a SAT Constancia

  1. Check document authenticity - Verify it's an official SAT document (check format and QR code if present)
  2. Validate RFC format - Ensure correct length and structure
  3. Verify active status - Status should show "Activo" (Active)
  4. Cross-reference legal name - Match with Acta Constitutiva and other documents
  5. Confirm fiscal address - Document as the canonical address
  6. Check document date - Should be recent (within 3 months for most KYC requirements)
  7. Review obligations - Note any compliance flags or issues

Extracting Data from SAT Constancia

  • Header section - Contains RFC and legal name
  • Address block - Fiscal address components

  • Obligations list - Filing requirements and schedules

Common Issues and Solutions

Expired or Old Constancia

Request a fresh constancia from the customer. The document should typically be no more than 3 months old for KYC purposes.

Status Shows Inactive

An inactive status may indicate tax issues. The customer should resolve their status with SAT before proceeding with business relationships.

Name Mismatch

The name on SAT Constancia may differ slightly from other documents due to formatting. Focus on key identifiers like RFC and fiscal address for verification.

Summary

The SAT Constancia de Situación Fiscal is essential for Mexican business KYC. It provides authoritative verification of:

  • RFC (tax ID) and legal name
  • Fiscal address (official registered address)
  • Tax regime and obligations
  • Active/inactive status

Always validate RFC format, check active status, and cross-reference information with other KYC documents for comprehensive verification.
